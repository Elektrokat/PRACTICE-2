Martin Luther King Jr.

...The Civil Rights Defender...


Here are some of his accomplishments:


  • 1929- Born in Atlanta, Georgia U.S.
  • 1955- Led the Montgomery Bus Boycott.
  • 1957- First President of SCLC.
  • 1957- Became the symbolic leader of African Americans.
  • 1963- Gave the famous "I have a dream speech".
  • 1963- Organised the great march on Washington.
  • 1964- Became Man of the year for Time Magazine.
  • 1964- Instigated the basic civil rights for African Americans.
  • 1964- Became the youngest recipient of the Nobel Peace prize.
  • 1968- Was assasinated by gunshot.

You can read more about him on his Wikipedia entry.

Indira Ghandi.

...The Iron Lady...


Here are some of her accomplishments:


  • 1917- Born in Prayagraj, India.
  • 1968- Facilitated the Green Revolution.
  • 1969- Nationalised 14 Private-Sector Banks.
  • 1971- Liberated Bangladesh.
  • 1971- Ended Privy Purse given to Royalty.
  • 1971- Facilitated India's Foreign Policy.
  • 1974- launched Smiling Buddha which was India's first nuclear bomb.
  • 1984- Sent the first Indian Rakesh Sharma into space.
  • 1984- Ordered the biggest military operation in India called Operation Blue Star.
  • 1984- Was assasinated by two of her body guards.

Nelson Mandela.

...The African Revolutionalist...


Here are some of his accomplishments:


  • 1918- Born in Mvezo, South Africa.
  • 1948- Was an opponent of the Apartheid Legislation.
  • 1950- Became the National President of the ANC Youth League.
  • 1952- He co-founded the first black legal partnership in South Africa.
  • 1961- Gave a great speech during the Rivonia trial.
  • 1964- Went to prison.
  • 1990- Was released from prison.
  • 1993- Was awarded a Noble Peace Prize.
  • 1994- Became the 1st black President of South Africa.
  • 2013- Died from a respiratory tract infection.
